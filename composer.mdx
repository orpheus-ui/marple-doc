The Composer is the bar pinned to the bottom of the Leads and Pipeline screens. Type a request in ordinary English and it does the equivalent clicking for you.

Press `⌘K` on a Mac or `Ctrl+K` on Windows to open and focus it from anywhere.

## What it can do

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Filter the table" icon="filter">
    _"show leads scoring 8 or higher"_, _"urgent leads from LinkedIn"_, _"anything from Acme"_. The table above updates in place and the Composer confirms what it applied.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Answer questions about a company" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    _"have we contacted Acme?"_ returns a small panel showing whether outreach went out, to which address, and when.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Summarise the pipeline" icon="chart-simple">
    _"what's the pipeline status?"_ returns counts by status as clickable tiles. Click any tile to jump to that slice of the queue.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Look up a specific lead" icon="file-magnifying-glass">
    Ask about one company and you get a compact card with its score, status, and role, plus a link into the queue.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Approve, reject, or regenerate" icon="circle-check">
    _"approve everything scoring 9 or above"_, _"regenerate that draft in a casual tone"_. These always ask for confirmation first.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Start a pipeline run" icon="play">
    _"run the pipeline"_ triggers a scrape, the same as clicking **Run now**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Anything that changes data asks first

Write actions, meaning approve, reject, regenerate, and trigger a run, never happen on the strength of your typed message alone. A confirmation dialog appears stating exactly what will happen and how many leads are involved. Nothing occurs until you click **Confirm**.

This is deliberate. Interpreting free text is imprecise, so the confirmation step is what stops a misread request from approving fifty leads you meant to exclude.

<Warning>
  Read the number in the confirmation dialog rather than assuming it matches what you asked for. If it looks wrong, cancel and either rephrase or use the filter controls directly.
</Warning>

## What it deliberately will not do

The Composer refuses several categories outright, and when it does you get a short line reading _"That's outside what I can help with here."_ No apology, no lecture.

It refuses:

- Anything touching API keys, passwords, cookies, or credentials
- Anything that would delete data
- Anything unrelated to lead management

The first two are security boundaries. The Composer cannot read or reveal a stored key even if you ask directly, and there is no way to phrase a request that gets around it. The third is just scope, it is not a general-purpose assistant and it will not write your Slack messages.

## Referring to what you are looking at

The Composer knows which screen you are on and which filters are active, so phrases like _"approve these"_ or _"reject that one"_ resolve against your current view. If the reference is genuinely ambiguous it will ask rather than guess.

## Practical notes

- Conversation history is kept only while the page is open. Refreshing clears it.
- The eraser icon clears the conversation without refreshing.
- Clicking the **Composer** label collapses and expands the panel.
- Filter requests change the page address, so the resulting view is bookmarkable exactly like a manually filtered one.

<Tip>
  The Composer is fastest for filters that would take several clicks, such as _"urgent leads scoring 7 or higher from Greenhouse"_. For a single filter, the controls are quicker. Use whichever suits the moment, they produce identical results.
</Tip>
