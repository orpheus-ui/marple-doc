This page assumes someone has already given you an account and sent you the Marple address. If not, ask whoever runs Marple at AssistWorld to invite you.

## Signing in

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Marple address in your browser">
    Bookmark it. You will be back daily.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your email and password">
    These are the credentials that came with your invite, not your regular AssistWorld login. They are separate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Sign in">
    You land directly on the Leads queue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your session stays signed in across page reloads and refreshes itself in the background. You do not need to log in every morning. To sign out deliberately, click your initials in the top-right corner and choose **Sign out**.
</Note>

If sign-in fails, see [Troubleshooting](/troubleshooting).

## Getting your bearings

Take thirty seconds to look at the bar across the top of the screen. From left to right:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The Marple logo" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Click it any time to return to the Leads queue.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Leads, Pipeline, and possibly Settings" icon="bars">
    Your three screens. If Settings is missing, you have a User account rather than super admin.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A small coloured dot with text beside it" icon="circle">
    The pipeline status indicator. Green means the last background run finished cleanly, amber and pulsing means a run is happening right now, red means the last run failed. The text tells you how long ago it ran. Click it for a panel with details and a **Run now** button.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A circle with your initials" icon="user">
    Your account menu. Light and dark theme options live here, along with **Sign out**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

At the bottom of the screen there is a narrow bar labelled **Composer** with a `⌘K` badge. That is the chat assistant. Ignore it for now, it gets its own page.

## Working your first lead

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a row with a green score badge">
    Scores of 8 or higher are green, 5 to 7 are amber, below 5 are red. Green means the AI thinks this is a strong prospect. Start with one of those.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click anywhere on the row">
    The row expands into a two-column panel. Do not click the small tick or cross icons on the right yet, those approve and reject immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the left column">
    You get the AI's assessment and one-sentence reasoning, any pain signals it spotted, the original job description, and the contact it found. There is a link to the original job posting if you want to see it in context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the right column">
    This is the draft email, with a subject line and a body. Both are editable, click into either and type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decide">
    If the email is good, click **Approve** at the bottom right. If the company is a bad fit, click **Reject**. If the email needs work, edit it first, click **Save edits**, then approve.
  </Step>
</Steps>

That is one lead. Repeat.

## What happens after you approve

Approving does not send anything. It moves the lead from **New** to **Approved** and makes it sendable.

To actually send, find the approved lead and click **Send**. The button only becomes active when the lead has both a contact email and a draft email. If either is missing, the button stays greyed out.

<Warning>
  Sending is real. The email leaves immediately and goes to a real person at a real company. There is no undo, no delay, and no draft folder to catch it. Read the draft before you click.
</Warning>

## If the queue is empty

Two possibilities.

The pipeline has not run yet. Click the status dot in the top right, then **Run now**. A run takes a minute or two. The dot turns amber while it works, and new leads appear on their own when it finishes.

Or you have filters applied that exclude everything. Look at the filter bar above the table. If there is a **Clear** button with a number badge, filters are active, click it to reset.
